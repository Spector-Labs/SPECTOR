Why now

The glasses era is arriving — fast.

Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses became the category's first mainstream hit. Google (Android XR), Samsung, and Apple are racing in behind them. For the first time, hundreds of millions of people are about to have a display in their field of view — and almost none of today's software was designed for it.

Hardware

It has shipped

Smart glasses left the lab. Ray-Ban Meta, XREAL, Viture and Brilliant Labs are in real customers' hands today — selling faster than anyone predicted.

Platforms

Big tech is all-in

Android XR, Samsung, and Apple are pouring resources into head-worn computing. The platform war for your field of view has officially started.

Software

It hasn't caught up

The apps are still phone apps. Whoever builds the native communication tools for glasses gets to define the category. That's the opening.

The problem

Reading a script still means looking away.

Every teleprompter today assumes a screen you stare at. On stage, on camera, or on a sales call, that means broken eye contact, a frozen "reading" voice, and notes you can't glance at without it being obvious. The hardware to fix this finally exists. The software didn't — until Spector.

The product

Not just another teleprompter.

Pacing

Adaptive speed

Speeds up and slows with your natural rhythm. Punctuation-aware timing, emphasis holds. No manual scroll ever.

Comfort

Spatial anchoring

Kalman-filtered head-tilt stabilizes text to reduce eye strain. Breathing animations create natural, immersive flow.

Analytics

Post-run intelligence

Avg WPM, pacing consistency, hesitation detection, and slowest-moment analysis after every rehearsal run.

Product vision

Where we're headed.

Cross-platform rehearsal analytics, native mobile player, and deep Ray-Ban Meta integration — the unified system we're building toward.

Comfort mode

Text that holds still while you don't.

Spatially anchored. Calm by design.

Standard teleprompters shake with every head movement — causing eye strain and that frozen "reading" look. Comfort mode locks your script in space so it stays stable and readable through an entire rehearsal.

How it works

From script to stage in three steps.

01

Paste your script

Drop a .txt file or type directly. Add emphasis and pause markers with one tap.

02

Rehearse on your phone

Adaptive pacing, Comfort spatial mode, and end-of-run analytics. Install as PWA for offline use.

03

Perform on glasses

Temple button controls advance, pause, and rewind. HUD-ready for Ray-Ban Meta, XREAL, Viture, and Brilliant Labs.
