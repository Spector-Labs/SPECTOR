For smart glasses (today & tomorrow)

Rehearse on your phone with library, auto-pacing, cues, and real analytics — then perform with more confidence on glasses. The right-temple button and touchpad on Ray-Ban Meta can control script advance/pause on supported HUD models.

  • Non-Display models (Gen 1/2 and equivalents): Phone companion rehearsal. Use haptics + audio feedback. Glasses handle camera/AI; SPECTOR runs on your phone for eyes-free practice.
  • Display / HUD models (Ray-Ban Meta Display and similar): Run the player directly via web app on the glasses' lens display for true heads-up teleprompting. Right temple button + touch controls (and future Neural Band gestures) for hands-free advance, pause, rewind.
  • Other brands (XREAL, Viture, Brilliant Labs, Even Realities, etc.): Broad compatibility via PWA on phone companion or any glasses that support web/browser apps. Different input surfaces (controllers, touch, voice) — SPECTOR focuses on the rehearsal engine.
  • In the player today: Comfort mode (subtle head-responsive movement + breathing), Focus, Presentation. Mirror for camera setups. **emphasis** and [pause] cues. Post-run stats on pacing and hesitations.
  • Future: Modular SpectorCore ready for native SDK ports and direct gesture mapping when platforms expose APIs.

Works with Ray-Ban Meta and any other smart-glasses workflow. No affiliation with Meta or Ray-Ban.
