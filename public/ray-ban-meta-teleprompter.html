The honest picture

Where Ray-Ban Meta stands — and where Spector fits.

Meta added a basic teleprompter to its $799 Ray-Ban Display glasses in 2026 — but it's advanced by hand (no auto-pacing), US-only, and locked to Meta's account and hardware. And the Gen 1 and Gen 2 Ray-Ban Meta glasses can't show text at all. So for most people, the practical answer is to rehearse on your phone until delivery is second nature, then speak with confidence. FAQ

Ray-Ban Meta teleprompter questions.

Does Ray-Ban Meta have a teleprompter?

Only the newer Ray-Ban Display model does, and it's basic: you advance the text by hand, it's US-only, and it needs a Meta account. The Gen 1 and Gen 2 glasses have no display, so they can't show text at all. Spector gives every Ray-Ban Meta owner a way to prepare — full adaptive rehearsal on your phone, free.

I have Gen 1 / Gen 2 (no display). Can I still use Spector?

Yes — that's exactly who it's for. You rehearse on your phone with adaptive pacing and analytics until the delivery is natural, then perform eyes-up. No screen in the glasses required.

Is Spector made by Meta?

No. Spector is an independent, open-source teleprompter and is not affiliated with Meta or Ray-Ban. It works across Ray-Ban Meta, XREAL, Viture, and more, so your prep isn't tied to one company.

Is it really free?

Yes — the core teleprompter is free forever, no watermark, no account. Try it right now.
