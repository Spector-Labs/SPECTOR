The gap

XREAL doesn't ship a teleprompter. Spector does.

XREAL's glasses are a display — they mirror your phone or PC onto a giant virtual screen, and XREAL's own software (Nebula) is winding down. There's no first-party teleprompter. So to read a script eyes-up on XREAL, you need a teleprompter that runs in that virtual screen. Spector is built for exactly that: an installable web app with adaptive, punctuation-aware pacing and cue markers — nothing to download from an app store.

FAQ

XREAL teleprompter questions.

Does XREAL have a built-in teleprompter?

No. XREAL glasses act as a virtual display for your phone or computer; there's no first-party teleprompter app, and XREAL's Nebula software is being phased out. To read a script eyes-up, you run a teleprompter on the virtual screen — which is exactly what Spector is designed for.

Which XREAL models work?

Any XREAL that shows your phone or PC screen — Air 2, One, and Pro — because Spector runs in the browser on that display. You can also rehearse the whole thing on just your phone before you ever put the glasses on.

Do I have to install anything?

No app store required. Spector is a PWA — open it in a browser and, if you like, install it to your home screen for offline use. The core is free and open source, so you can read exactly what it does.

Is it really free?

Yes — the core teleprompter is free forever. There's no watermark and no account. Try it right now.
